Sleep Architecture Protocol

Deep sleep optimization through peptide-mediated GABA modulation, cortisol regulation, and circadian entrainment.

Protocol Overview

The Sleep Architecture Protocol is designed for individuals seeking to optimize their sleep quality, depth, and overall architecture. Unlike conventional sleep aids that simply induce drowsiness, this protocol targets the underlying neurochemical pathways regulating sleep cycles, including GABAergic signaling, cortisol rhythm, and melatonin synthesis.

Based on current peptide research and clinical applications, the protocol emphasizes non-addictive, short-intervention strategies that can restore natural sleep patterns within 4-8 weeks when combined with proper sleep hygiene.

Primary Goals

  • Increase deep sleep (N3) — Enhance slow-wave sleep duration for physical repair and glymphatic clearance
  • Optimize REM architecture — Support cognitive consolidation and emotional regulation
  • Regulate cortisol rhythm — Lower nocturnal cortisol and support healthy HPA axis function
  • Improve sleep latency — Reduce time to fall asleep without pharmaceutical sedation
